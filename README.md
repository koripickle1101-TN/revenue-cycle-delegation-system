## Project Purpose

Healthcare administration teams do not need more random prompting. They need repeatable delegation systems.

This project models how fictional prior authorization workflow data can be reviewed by specialized AI assistant roles to identify:

- Missing eligibility information
- Documentation gaps
- Delayed payer responses
- Unclear escalation ownership
- Authorization mismatch risk
- Claim readiness concerns
- Upstream denial prevention opportunities

The goal is not to replace healthcare staff. The goal is to make hidden workflow risk easier to see, prioritize, and prevent before it becomes denial, delay, rework, or patient frustration.

## Featured Workflow

### Prior Authorization Delay Prevention

Workflow studied:

1. Patient scheduled
2. Insurance captured
3. Eligibility verified
4. Service reviewed for authorization requirement
5. Clinical documentation checked
6. Authorization submitted
7. Payer response tracked
8. Missing information escalated
9. Approval documented
10. Claim readiness confirmed

## Core Insight

Most revenue cycle breakdowns do not begin at billing. They begin upstream when intake, documentation, authorization, payer response tracking, or handoff ownership breaks down.

AI-supported delegation can help surface those risks earlier by turning workflow tracker data into prioritized action summaries for human review.
